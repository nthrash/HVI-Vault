# Heart Failure ICU Nurse Practitioner Orientation Pathway

**Unit Scope:** Heart Transplant, High Risk CABG/PCI, Valves, Lung Transplant, PH.
**Device Scope:** LVAD (HM2/3, HW), Impella (CP, 5.5, RP Flex), RVAD (CentriMag, Protek Duo), ECMO.
**Timeline:** 3–6 Months (Adjustable based on prior experience).

---

## Phase 1: The Foundation (Weeks 1–4)
**Focus:** Unit logistics, standard Post-Op physiology, basic hemodynamics, and "Bread & Butter" Heart Failure.

| Week | Clinical Focus | Procedural Goals | Self-Study / Education Topics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | **Unit Immersion**<br>• Workflow, rounding structure, EMR navigation.<br>• Intro to standard Post-Op CABG/Valve protocols.<br>• Admission/Discharge criteria. | • **Observation only.**<br>• Locate all crash carts, airway boxes, and sterile supply rooms. | • **Physiology:** Frank-Starling curve & P-V Loops (Normal vs. HF).<br>• **Pharm:** Standard vasoactive gtt titrations (Levo, Vaso, Epi, Milrinone, Dobutamine). |
| **2** | **Heart Failure Fundamentals**<br>• Right vs. Left heart failure presentations.<br>• Volume optimization: Diuretics, Aquapheresis basics.<br>• PA Catheter interpretation (Numbers vs. Waveforms). | • Setup for A-lines and CVCs.<br>• Ultrasound distinct knobs/competency.<br>• **Sim Lab:** Radial A-line mechanics. | • **Physiology:** Forrester Classification.<br>• **Topic:** Pulmonary Hypertension groups 1–5.<br>• **Reading:** ISHLT Guidelines for Acute HF management. |
| **3** | **Cardiogenic Shock (CS)**<br>• SCAI Stages of Shock (A-E).<br>• Intro to Temporary MCS: **Impella CP**. | • Perform 1–2 Radial Art Lines (Supervised).<br>• Observe Central Line placement. | • **Device:** Impella physics (placement signals, purge pressure, hemolysis monitoring).<br>• **Pharm:** Inotropes vs. Pressors in CS. |
| **4** | **Valves & Rhythm**<br>• Post-TAVR/SAVR management.<br>• Pacemaker basics (sensitivity, output, thresholds). | • Perform Central Line (IJ/Fem) (Supervised).<br>• Pacing wire removal competency. | • **Device:** Temporary Pacemaker modes (DDD, VVI, AAI).<br>• **Topic:** Atrial Fibrillation management in the ICU (Amio vs. Digoxin vs. Cardioversion). |

## Phase 2: MCS Deep Dive (Weeks 5–12)
**Focus:** Device specific physiology (LVAD/RVAD), advanced hemodynamics, and escalating procedural independence.

| Week | Clinical Focus | Procedural Goals | Self-Study / Education Topics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **5-6** | **Durable LVADs (HM3, HM2, HW)**<br>• Speed changes vs. flow estimation.<br>• PI events, suction events, low flow alarms.<br>• MAP goals and Doppler BP measurement. | • Independent Radial Art Lines.<br>• Supervised Femoral Art Lines.<br>• Supervised Dialysis Catheter placement. | • **Device:** HeartMate 3 "Artificial Pulse" vs. HeartWare waveform.<br>• **Complications:** DRI (Drive Line Infection), GI Bleeds in LVADs, RHF post-implant. |
| **7-8** | **Advanced Temp MCS (Left Sided)**<br>• **Impella 5.5**: Axillary approach management, positional alarms.<br>• Weaning protocols for Impella. | • Independent CVC placement.<br>• Intro to Bronchoscopy setup/assist (if applicable). | • **Topic:** Differential diagnosis of "Low Flow" on Impella (Position vs. Volume vs. RV Failure).<br>• **Pharm:** Anticoagulation for MCS (Purge solutions, Heparin nomograms, bivalirudin). |
| **9-10**| **The Right Ventricle & RVADs**<br>• **Protek Duo & Impella RP Flex**.<br>• Diagnosing RV failure (CVP/PCWP ratios, PAPi).<br>• Managing RV afterload (iNO, Milrinone). | • Independent Dialysis Catheter placement.<br>• Arterial access for ECMO (Simulation/Observation). | • **Physiology:** RV Pressure-Volume loops.<br>• **Device:** Protek Duo cannula positioning & migration risks.<br>• **Topic:** Heparin Induced Thrombocytopenia (HIT) management. |
| **11-12**| **Transplant Immunology & Management**<br>• Induction therapy (Basiliximab/Thymo).<br>• Acute Rejection surveillance.<br>• Denervated heart physiology (Atropine ineffectiveness). | • Procedural consolidation (Aim for 10+ lines logged).<br>• Vent management: Weaning parameters. | • **Topic:** ISHLT Grading for Rejection (1R/2R/3R).<br>• **Pharm:** Tacrolimus/Cyclosporine/MMF pharmacology and interactions. |

## Phase 3: High Acuity & Independence (Weeks 13–24)
**Focus:** ECMO, Multiorgan failure (CRRT/CVVH), Emergency Codes, and Full Patient Load.

### Weeks 13–16: ECMO & Rescue Therapies
* **Clinical:** VA vs. VV ECMO indications. **CentriMag** console management. North-South syndrome (Harlequin) in VA ECMO.
* **Procedures:** Assist with ECMO cannulation. Manage large bore access sites.
* **Study:** ELSO Guidelines. Vent management on ECMO (Rest settings).

### Weeks 17–20: Synthesis & Emergency Management
* **Clinical:** Managing "Crash and Burn" patients (e.g., Post-cardiotomy shock). Coding patients (ACLS leadership).
* **Procedures:** Independent management of complications (e.g., accidental line pulls, bleeding).
* **Study:** Crisis Resource Management (CRM). Review mock code scenarios for LVAD patients (Check line, controller, power).

### Weeks 21–24: Graduation Phase
* **Milestone:** Full patient load (matched to unit standard).
* **Evaluation:** "Check-off" on all devices (Written test + Bedside troubleshooting).
* **Goal:** The NP functions as a resource for bedside RNs.

## Competency Checklists

### 1. Device "Driver's License"
The orientee must demonstrate bedside competency (troubleshooting and numbers) for the following:
- [ ] **Impella 5.5:** Purge change, calculated flow interpretation, position troubleshooting.
- [ ] **Protek Duo / RP Flex:** Inflow/outflow ports, migration signs, securing the device.
- [ ] **HeartMate 3:** Controller exchange, backup battery test, interpreting log files/events.
- [ ] **CentriMag:** Hand-cranking procedure, air embolism intervention.

### 2. Procedural Log Goals
*(Adjust numbers based on unit volume)*
- [ ] **Radial Arterial Lines:** 10 Successful
- [ ] **Femoral Arterial Lines:** 5 Successful
- [ ] **Central Venous Catheters:** 10 Successful
- [ ] **Dialysis Catheters:** 5 Successful
